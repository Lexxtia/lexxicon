History

Miscellaneous Things About Alex

birth-and-origin On November 13th, 1980 I was born Alejandro Reyes in a small town in southern California called Oxnard, mainly populated with a growing Hispanic community.

family-structure I grew up in a house alongside three uncles, my mother, and my grandmother. My father lived with his other family with his wife and children.

father-history At the time, my father was dating my mother in secrecy, then found out about his other life where he said he would get divorced to be with us, but did not happen.

father-figures The only father figures I had were my three uncles that lived with us. I would occasionally see my father here and there as I was growing up.

father-memories The only thing I remember about his visits were us either going to a restaurant, buying me video games, maybe the occasional drive in theatre, and some camping trips. He would try as much as he could to be involved in my upbringing, at least I would like to think so.

upbringing Even though my uncles were my father figures, I was mainly brought up by my mother and grandmother, as well as many aunts that would visit the house.

discipline I was taught how to be gentle, respect my elders, and lots of discipline. I wasn’t exactly a great case, I was very defiant, rowdy, and caused a lot of mischief, despite all best efforts of those raising me.

punishment As a result, I was punished pretty often using old school conventional means such as spankings, often with objects such as a belt, or a broom, or anything able enough to inflict some pain.

fear-and-anger I don’t think any of it ever really worked other than to become afraid of the object itself, and not the cause or effect of the action that brought us to the conclusion. At a certain age, doing those actions to me became counterproductive. I would become angered, or enraged at the action resulting in me fighting back becoming increasingly unstable.

loss-and-consequence Instead of using physical violence, taking things away from me were the next thing to try. Those lost items actually started to make a connection with all of the problems I caused and started to make an impact in my decision making.

nintendo As soon as something I learned to like got taken away, I would become sad and started a pattern of imploding. An example I can think of that happened over and over again was taking away my Nintendo by hiding the console, or the controllers.

circumventing-punishment I couldn’t get home from school and start hammering away, so I ended up starting to invite friends that had the missing pieces of the console over. My mother started getting wind that suddenly I wasn’t inviting people over to do school work, but to circumvent her process of punishment.

grounding-and-gameboy That led to other types of discipline such as grounding me for long periods at a time. Luckily the Gameboy was invented around that time that made things easier to stay in one room while being grounded, although she didn’t know that I had one, I hid it so well that even today I don’t think she knows.

lying I became increasingly efficient at lying, and telling fabricated stories of the truth, anything to keep my lifestyle intact.

mother-breakdown Well one day my mother found out about my constant lies and deceit that she must have ran out of options to try and discipline me that she broke down and cried. It was the first time I’ve ever seen her do that, and it wasn’t some attempt to get me to stop, she had literally broke down because nothing worked.

turning-point I was old enough at the time to understand everything she was trying and hoping the best for me. She said that she wasn’t doing this just to punish me, but to try and raise me to be a better person than my father was to her. I was in such shock that I started crying too, and apologizing to her for everything I’ve done and that I would try to be a better person from that point on.

meeting-daisy All of these new changes came at the right time when I lost my stomach meeting a girl that moved in next door named Daisy.

love-at-first-sight At the time of my life, it felt like love at first sight and nothing else on earth mattered other than to meet and hopefully go out with this girl.

looking-for-her I would peek out of my living room window to see if she was in her backyard, or outside her front yard. I would also go into my backyard and act like I was doing something out there for a purpose in hopes to be able to see her through the backyard fence.

watching-for-her The only other way to see her would be to sit in my front yard and watch her come and go from either school, or when her parents drive her somewhere, though I would have to be there at the right moment to even know when that happened. My entire life was dedicated to finding a way to meet her.

first-conversation She surprised me one day while I was playing with my dog by climbing on top of the fence and asking me if the ball she was holding in her hands was mine.

first-impression I froze and didn’t know what to say since it was the first time we saw each other eye to eye. I forgot I was playing with the dog when it jumped on me threw me to the floor. We were both laughing pretty hard at the misfortune, but were soon talking about random things that had nothing to do with anything, but that didn’t matter.

euphoria I felt like I was riding in clouds, floating in air, hearing sounds that weren’t even heard by anybody else other than myself.

growing-connection From that day on, we were making excuses to talk to each other. She would be outside a lot more often than she first was, and I would make any excuse to play outside, or be outside.

ages At the time, I was 17 and she was 14.

first-real-feelings She was the first girl I’ve ever cared about to like. I’ve met other girls before her, but for some reason none of them hit me quite like she did.

what-drew-me-to-her Maybe it was her smile, maybe the way she walked, maybe her voice, maybe her hair, I don’t quite remember, but none the less she would be my first attempt at trying my charm with another person.
