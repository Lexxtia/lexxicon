Miscellaneous Things About Alex

On November 13th, 1980 I was born Alejandro Reyes in a small town in southern California called Oxnard, mainly populated with a growing Hispanic community.

I grew up in a house alongside three uncles, my mother, and my grandmother. My father lived with his other family with his wife and children.

At the time, my father was dating my mother in secrecy, then found out about his other life where he said he would get divorced to be with us, but did not happen.

The only father figures I had were my three uncles that lived with us. I would occasionally see my father here and there as I was growing up.

Even though my uncles were my father figures, I was mainly brought up by my mother and grandmother, as well as many aunts that would visit the house.

I was taught how to be gentle, respect my elders, and lots of discipline. I wasn’t exactly a great case — I was very defiant, rowdy, and caused a lot of mischief.

Discipline methods evolved over time, from physical punishment to taking things away, which ultimately had a stronger emotional impact on me.

I became increasingly efficient at lying and maintaining my lifestyle, until one day my mother broke down crying — a moment that deeply changed me.

I realized everything she was trying to do was to raise me to be better than my father had been to her. That moment reshaped me.

Around that time, everything shifted again when I met Daisy.

I became completely focused on her — watching for her, hoping to run into her, finding any excuse to see her.

The first time we spoke, everything felt surreal — like I was floating.

From that point on, we found ways to keep talking. She was 14, I was 17. She was the first girl I ever truly felt something real for.
